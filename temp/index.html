Outdoor Water Use 

 

Irrigation

Many people who water outdoors can benefit from controlled irrigation.  Automatic sprinkler systems allow for regular, beneficial watering of vegetation according to the specific needs of your yard..   

While watering early or late in the day will minimize loss due to evaporation, evening watering can encourage fungus growth.  Early morning watering gives your plants protection from the hot summer sun before competition for the day's greatest water needs.

 

Water Harvesting with Rainwater barrels

 

Rainwater harvesting captures, diverts, and stores rainwater for later use.

 

Rainwater barrels are an easy way to collect and reuse rain water that would ordinarily run off.  Rain barrels are more attractive than ever, and can be purchased or easily made.

 

Approximately 550 gallons of rainwater can be collected for every 1000 square feet of collection surface per inch of rain.

 

Do-it-yourself stores, such as Home Depot and Lowes now carry kits that allow you to easily and inexpensively construct your own rainbarrel from a 55-gallon drum.

  

Vegetation Choices

Vegetation around your home or property isn't just for asthetics.  The right vegetation can help shade your home, reducing cooling costs.   It can reduce or eliminate the effects of erosion.  It can provide a habitat for native, beneficial animals. Without proper planning, though, your choice of vegetation can be water wasteful. 

 

When planning your landscaping, select vegetation suitable for your area.  Reginally appropriate, native plants may be much cheaper to sustain in the long run than water thirsty ones.  The EPA has The WaterSense Water-Smart Landscapes guide (14 pp, 2.3 MB, About PDF) to help you get started in developing a water-smart landscape for your home or property.

 

The EPA's WaterSense Water Budget Tool can help select landscape plants that are water efficient and tell you if you have designed a landscape that will use an appropriate amount of water for your climate.
